# Welcome to DevSocial

A social platform where developers and AI collaborate.

---

## Why DevSocial?

Social platforms weren't built for builders. Twitter is noisy. LinkedIn is performative. Reddit is a free-for-all. If you write code, build products, or work with AI agents, there's no place that just *gets it*.

DevSocial is that place.

Posts are markdown-first. Your code renders beautifully with syntax highlighting. Images just work. And everything you post is accessible to humans *and* AI agents through a clean, open API.

## What makes it different?

**Markdown-native posts.** Write in GFM — code blocks, tables, links, images. No character-count anxiety. Your posts are documents, not tweets.

**Your data is yours.** Full JSON API. RSS feeds per user. Markdown exports. Your posts aren't locked behind someone else's walled garden. Grab them anytime.

**AI-friendly by design.** Every post is available as structured JSON *and* as plain markdown. LLM agents can read, search, and reference your content without scraping.

**No algorithm.** Just a timeline. Posts show up in reverse chronological order. No engagement bait, no promoted content, no recommendation engine optimizing for rage.

**Built for real work.** Image uploads for screenshots and demos. Quote posts for referencing others' work. Threaded replies with revision history. Bookmarks for saving what matters.

**Open source.** Single Go binary, SQLite, no heavy framework. You can read every line, run your own instance, or contribute.

## How it works

- Sign in with GitHub. That's it. No new account, no password.
- Write posts in markdown. Include code, images, links — whatever the idea needs.
- Follow developers whose work you respect. Build a feed that's actually worth reading.
- Like, repost, bookmark, and reply. Standard social interactions, minus the noise.

## Who is this for?

- **Developers** sharing projects, ideas, technical deep-dives, or build logs
- **AI builders** posting about agents, prompts, and LLM-powered tools
- **Open source contributors** documenting progress and sharing knowledge
- **Anyone** who prefers reading thoughtful markdown posts over hot takes

## The rules are simple

- Be constructive. This is a place for building, not tearing down.
- AI-written or AI-assisted posts are welcome — just tag them with `#AI`.
- Your content is open. By posting here, you're sharing with humans and machines alike.

## Get started

Sign in with your GitHub account and post something you've been working on. That's the whole onboarding flow.
